# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Engineers and peers — fellow developers who arrive to read posts about Go, backend systems, microservices, and AI agents, or to check out who's behind them. They're reading in short focused sessions (often from a link on social/aggregators), and they should walk away with a sense of technical credibility and calm expertise. Secondary: anyone exploring the wider hovanhoa.net subdomain ecosystem (insight, gallery, music, status, info).

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio + blog for Hồ Văn Hòa (hovanhoa), a software engineer focused on backend systems and AI agents. The site is the hub of a family of subdomains and the home for long-form technical writing. Success: posts are effortless to read, the site feels unmistakably *his*, and visitors leave with a clear sense of who he is and what he cares about.

## Brand Personality

Warm, personal, curious — expressed through a restrained editorial base. The current identity (lowercase text everywhere, graphite ink, single orange accent, Geist sans/mono, terminal-flavored details) stays as the foundation; warmth comes in gradually through voice and copy, the orange accent, and small personal touches — not through a louder visual system.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS landing page**: gradient heroes, metric cards, marketing-speak, corporate startup energy.
- **Over-designed showcase**: heavy animations, 3D, scroll-jacking — style overpowering the writing.
- The generic dev-portfolio template (hero + skills grid + project cards) is also not the model, though this is a softer avoid than the two above.

## Design Principles

1. **The writing is the product** — every design decision serves readability and the reader's focus; decoration that competes with prose loses.
2. **Quiet base, warm details** — editorial restraint by default; personality enters through copy, the orange accent, and small intentional touches (the blinking caret, the ecosystem graph), never a wholesale louder system.
3. **Dev-native, not dev-clichéd** — terminal and monospace flavor is identity, but used sparingly and precisely, not as a costume.
4. **One ecosystem, one voice** — the `--rd-*` token layer is shared across the subdomain family; changes here should keep the family coherent.
5. **Lowercase is the voice** — the all-lowercase treatment is a deliberate brand signature, not a bug to fix.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: ≥4.5:1 contrast for body text, ≥3:1 for large text; `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation; full keyboard navigability. The lowercase transform is visual only (CSS `text-transform`), so screen readers receive normal-case source text — keep it that way.
